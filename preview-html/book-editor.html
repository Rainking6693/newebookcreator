Chapter 5: The Confrontation

3,240 words • Last edited 2 hours ago

Detective Sarah Mitchell stood at the threshold of the manor's grand library, her hand trembling slightly as she reached for the ornate brass handle. The events of the past three days had led her to this moment—a confrontation that would either solve the mystery of Blackwood Manor or claim her as its next victim.

The heavy oak door creaked open, revealing a room shrouded in shadows despite the afternoon sun streaming through tall windows. Dust motes danced in the golden light, and the musty smell of old books filled her nostrils. But it wasn't the familiar scent of aged paper that made her heart race—it was the figure seated behind the mahogany desk.

"I've been expecting you, Detective," came the voice from the shadows. Lord Blackwood himself sat motionless, his piercing blue eyes fixed on her with an intensity that made her skin crawl. "Though I must admit, I hoped it wouldn't come to this."

Sarah stepped into the room, her hand instinctively moving to the service weapon at her hip. "Lord Blackwood. We need to talk about the night Lady Catherine disappeared."

A cold smile played at the corners of his mouth. "Ah yes, my dear departed wife. Such a tragic accident, wouldn't you agree?" His voice carried a mocking tone that sent chills down Sarah's spine.

"That's where you're wrong," Sarah replied, her voice steady despite the fear coursing through her veins. "It wasn't an accident, and we both know it. The evidence doesn't lie, Lord Blackwood. The blood spatter patterns, the missing jewelry, the forged suicide note—it all points to murder."

Lord Blackwood's composure began to crack. His fingers drummed nervously on the desk, and Sarah noticed a slight twitch in his left eye. "You have no proof," he said, but his voice lacked its earlier confidence.

"Actually, I do." Sarah reached into her jacket pocket and withdrew a small evidence bag containing a distinctive cufflink. "We found this in the garden, near where Lady Catherine's body was discovered. It matches the one you're wearing right now."
